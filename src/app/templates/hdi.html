HDI vs % Urban Population:

This plot shows the relationship of HDI and % of urban population of a country for a given time period. We can see that the countries that are more urbanized are also more developed.


HDI, GII and GDP per Capita

This plot shows the relationship of HDI, GII and GDP per Capita of a country for a given time period. We can see that the HDI is consistantly higher when the GDP per Capita is more than 10,000 and other factors play a role in the HDI of countries with lower GDP per Capita. We also see that the Gender Inequality Index is higher for countries with a lower GDP per capita and lower HDI. These parameters show a stronger relationship for more developed countries.


GDP VS HDI

This plot shows the relationshipt of Gross Domestic Product to Human Development Index. Somewhat predictably, HDI rises with GDP. Turns out countries with more money are able to develop more.
